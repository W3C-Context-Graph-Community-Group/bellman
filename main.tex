\begin{abstract}
The Bellman equation is the foundation of sequential decision-making in AI, 
economics, and control theory. It has one precondition that has never been 
explicitly formalized: that the decision-maker can observe the full state 
relevant to its decision.

We identify a candidate bound on boundary uncertainty that is to 
multi-agent decision systems what channel capacity is to communication 
systems: a quantity derivable before any agent runs that determines 
whether reliable decision-making across the boundary is achievable. 
Whether this bound is tight --- whether $H_{\text{total}}$ bits of 
provenance is both necessary and sufficient to prevent Bellman failure 
--- is the open question whose resolution would complete the structural 
parallel to information theory.

We introduce a four-category scoring framework for agent behavior at 
serialization boundaries: \textbf{Asked} ($A$), \textbf{Detected} ($D$), 
\textbf{Undetected} ($U$), \textbf{Hallucinated} ($H$). A critical 
non-obvious result: $D$ and $U$ are behaviorally distinct at Layer~1 but 
produce identical epistemic consequences at Layer~2 --- both result in 
null uncertainty. Only $A$ prevents null uncertainty. $H$ is strictly 
worse: it expands the downstream state space with fictitious dimensions 
over which the Bellman equation optimizes, and no rotation can repair 
it because no ground truth exists anywhere in the pipeline.

We provide a minimal empirical protocol with a hardcoded answer key 
computable before any agent runs: 7.22 bits of boundary entropy, 4 
required cross-boundary measurements, 150 indistinguishable 
configurations. We show the failure is not fixable by improving the 
agent, because the information needed to detect null dimensions is on 
the other side of the boundary. We pose open questions including 
whether a tight lower bound on cross-boundary provenance exists that 
would complete the structural parallel to Shannon's noisy coding theorem.
\end{abstract}

\section{Where Bellman Breaks: Scope and Motivation}
\label{sec:motivation}
%% ============================================================

The Bellman equation is embedded in every reinforcement learning 
algorithm, every dynamic programming solution, and every AI agent 
that plans over multiple steps. Its precondition --- that the agent 
can observe the state relevant to its decision --- is so fundamental 
it is rarely stated. This paper is about what happens when that 
precondition is not merely violated by noise or partial observation, 
but structurally destroyed by the mechanism through which agents 
communicate.

In enterprise systems handling high-volume settlements, educational 
technology platforms serving millions of users, and organizations with 
hundreds of integrated systems, codebook mismatches at serialization 
boundaries are a persistent source of operational failure measured in 
months and years of engineering time. The mismatch is never detected 
by the systems involved. It surfaces only when a human asks what a 
column means. This paper makes that question formal, computable, and 
engineerable.

When agents operate in pipelines --- one agent's output becoming the 
next agent's input --- each serialization boundary is a point at which 
the precondition can fail silently. The downstream agent receives 
well-formed data, passes all syntactic validation, and optimizes 
confidently over a state space that may be simultaneously missing real 
dimensions and populated with fictitious ones. No internal diagnostic 
detects this. The value function converges --- to the wrong number.

The W3C Context Graph Community Group is developing formal 
infrastructure to make this failure mode measurable and engineerable, 
in the same way Shannon's information theory made communication 
capacity measurable~\cite{shannon1948}. This paper establishes the 
mathematical boundary conditions.

\section{Background: The Bellman Equation}
%% ============================================================

The Bellman equation expresses the principle of optimality:
\[
V^*(s) = \max_{a \in A} \left[ R(s, a) + \gamma \sum_{s'} 
P(s' | s, a)\, V^*(s') \right]
\]
where $V^*(s)$ is the value of state $s$ under the optimal policy. 
The equation has one precondition: \emph{the agent must be able to 
observe $s$}. This paper is about what happens when that precondition 
fails at the boundaries between systems where data crosses but 
codebooks do not.


%% ============================================================
\section{The Precondition}
%% ============================================================

\begin{definition}[True State]
At a system boundary, the true state is $s_{\mathrm{true}} = (s, \omega)$, 
where $s$ is the data the agent receives and $\omega \in \Omega$ is the 
codebook configuration.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Observation Function]
The observation function at a system boundary is $O(s, \omega) = s$. 
The data crosses the boundary. The codebook does not.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Resolution Capacity]
The resolution capacity $R$ is the number of configurations in $\Omega$ 
that the agent can distinguish from $s$ alone. For a bare value with no 
metadata, $R = 1$.
\end{definition}

\noindent When $|\Omega| > R$, the pigeonhole principle guarantees that 
at least $|\Omega| - R$ configurations produce identical observations.

\section{The Observability Condition}
\label{sec:observability}
%% ============================================================

\begin{lemma}[Bellman Observability Failure]
\label{thm:bellman}
If:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item there exist $\omega_1, \omega_2 \in \Omega$ such that the 
  optimal action differs:
  $\arg\max_a R(s, \omega_1, a) \neq \arg\max_a R(s, \omega_2, a)$,
  \item $O(s, \omega_1) = O(s, \omega_2) = s$,
\end{enumerate}
then the observed value function $\hat{V}(s) \neq V^*(s, \omega)$ 
for at least one $\omega$, and no function computable from $s$ alone 
can restore equality.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Different optimal actions yield different optimal values: 
$V^*(s, \omega_1) \neq V^*(s, \omega_2)$. But $\hat{V}(s)$ returns 
one number. One number cannot equal two different values. Every 
function of $s$ returns the same output for $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$ 
because $O$ projects away $\omega$. \qed
\end{proof}

The divergence gap is:
$\Delta = |V^*(s, \omega_1) - V^*(s, \omega_2)| > 0$.

This is the \emph{closure property}: no function computable within 
a fixed state space $S$ recovers a variable $\omega \notin S$. The 
closure property is the load-bearing claim of this paper. Everything 
that follows is downstream of it.

\section{The Universal Fault Line}
\label{sec:fault-line}
%% ============================================================

The serialization boundary is not a niche result in decision theory. 
The following frameworks each have a precondition that fails at the 
same structural point. These are not analogies. They are the same 
structural failure --- projection of a shared variable without 
reporting the severance --- expressed in different formalisms.

\textbf{Bellman / dynamic programming:} The observability 
precondition. At the serialization boundary, the codebook $\omega$ 
is projected away. The value function converges to the wrong number 
(Lemma~\ref{thm:bellman}).

\textbf{Nash equilibrium / game theory:} The common knowledge 
precondition. At the serialization boundary, the codebook is not 
common knowledge. The Nash equilibrium computed over misaligned 
codebooks is not an equilibrium of the actual game.

\textbf{Kuramoto / synchronization theory:} The coupling coherence 
precondition. At the serialization boundary, the codebook is not in 
the signal. No coupling force acts on it 
(Proposition~\ref{prop:strogatz}).

\textbf{Kalman filtering:} The observation model precondition. If 
the codebook for the measurement differs from the codebook for the 
state, the Kalman gain is computed over the wrong model. The filter 
converges. Every diagnostic says the filter is healthy.

\textbf{Shannon channel capacity:} Shannon explicitly scoped his 
theory to reliable transmission, not semantics. The capacity theorem 
tells you how many bits can cross the channel. It does not tell you 
whether the bits mean the same thing on both sides.

The closure property applies identically to all: no function 
computable from the received data alone recovers the projected 
variable. The bound $H_{\text{total}} = \sum_i \log_2 |\Omega_i|$ 
measures the same fault at every boundary, in every domain.

\textbf{Scope.} The Bellman failure (Lemma~\ref{thm:bellman}) and 
the Kuramoto failure (Proposition~\ref{prop:strogatz}) are derived 
rigorously in this paper. The Nash, Kalman, and Shannon applications 
follow the same structural pattern and are stated here as conjectured 
applications; full derivations are future work 
(Section~\ref{sec:metamath}).

\section{The Strogatz Inversion}
\label{sec:strogatz}
%% ============================================================

Strogatz (2003) proved that coupled oscillators synchronize 
through a shared medium. The Kuramoto model:
\[
\dot{\theta}_i = \omega_i + \frac{K}{N} \sum_{j=1}^{N} 
\sin(\theta_j - \theta_i)
\]
Above critical coupling $K_c$, the system locks. Synchronization 
is passive --- the phase \emph{is} the signal.

At system boundaries, the variable that must synchronize is 
\textbf{not in the signal}.

\begin{proposition}[Strogatz Inversion]
\label{prop:strogatz}
Let $(X, Y, p(x), p(y|x))$ be a Shannon channel between systems 
$A$ and $B$ with codebooks $C_A, C_B$ and divergence 
$\delta_{AB} = d(C_A, C_B)$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item No quantity computable from the channel varies with 
  $\delta_{AB}$.
  \item The channel dynamics contain no coupling force on the codebook.
  \item $\delta_{AB}$ does not converge under data exchange alone, 
  regardless of bandwidth, duration, or volume.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
$C_A$ and $C_B$ do not appear in $(X, Y, p(x), p(y|x))$. Every 
channel quantity is a function of $p(x,y)$, which is identical 
whether $C_A = C_B$ or $C_A \neq C_B$. No channel observable 
couples to $\delta_{AB}$. \qed
\end{proof}

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
& \textbf{Kuramoto (physical)} & \textbf{Boundary (semantic)} \\
\midrule
Variable to sync & Phase $\theta_i$ & Codebook $C_i$ \\
In the signal? & Yes & No \\
Coupling force & $\frac{K}{N}\sin(\theta_j - \theta_i)$ & None \\
Convergence & Spontaneous above $K_c$ & Does not occur \\
Recovery & Passive (listen) & Active (rotation) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{The defensive dual.} If semantic alignment cannot propagate 
passively, then semantic contamination cannot propagate passively 
either. The serialization boundary functions as a semantic firewall: 
null interference is the dual of null uncertainty.

\[
H_{x+1}^{\text{inh}} = \sum_{k=1}^{x} H_k^{\text{own}}

The agent at layer $L$ sees only $H_L^{\text{own}}$. It is blind to

\[
|\Omega_{\text{total}}^{(x)}| = \prod_{k=1}^{x} \prod_{y=1}^{n_k}

\[
H_{\text{strategy}} \leq T \cdot \sum_{k=1}^{L} H_k^{\text{own}}

$T = 50$, $L = 2$, $H_1^{\text{own}} = 7.22$, 
$H_2^{\text{own}} \approx 5$:
$H_{\text{strategy}} \leq 611$ bits.

\section{Five Cases at Every Boundary}
\label{sec:five-cases}
%% ============================================================

Every serialization boundary operates in exactly one of five 
verification regimes. Not choosing is Case~1.

Let $t_k$ be verification times, $\tau_k = t_{k+1} - t_k$ the 
interval, and $\dot{\delta}_{AB}$ the codebook drift rate.

\textbf{Case 1 --- Absent.} $\tau_k = \infty$. No verification. 
Null uncertainty from the first crossing.

\textbf{Case 2 --- Snapshot.} One verification at $t_0$, then 
$\tau_k = \infty$. Someone wrote a data dictionary once. It lives 
in a wiki. No one has checked since.

\textbf{Case 3 --- Intermittent.} $\tau_k = T$ for fixed $T > 0$. 
Drift bounded by $\dot{\delta}_{AB} \cdot T$, but the bound is not 
transmitted with the data.

\textbf{Case 4 --- On Request.} Triggered by receiver query. By the 
closure property, the receiver cannot query for dimensions they do 
not know are missing. $U$ and $H$ fields generate no query signal. 
On-request verification resolves known unknowns only.

\textbf{Case 5 --- Continuous.} $\tau_k \to 0$. Verification at 
every crossing. The only case where uncertainty is genuinely resolved.

\textbf{The critical asymmetry:} Case~4 is epistemically more 
dangerous than Case~3. It produces false confidence --- the receiver 
asked, got an answer, and proceeds confidently. But they only asked 
about what they knew to ask about. The confidence is the danger.

\section{The Reality Chain}
\label{sec:reality-chain}

The serialization boundary extends from physical sensors to human 
experience:

\[
\underset{\text{analog}}{\text{World}}
\;\xrightarrow{\;C_{\text{sensor}}\;}
\;\underset{\text{digital}}{\text{Signal}}
\;\xrightarrow{\;C_{\text{model}}\;}
\;\underset{\text{digital}}{\text{State}}
\;\xrightarrow{\;C_{\text{agent}}\;}
\;\underset{\text{digital}}{\text{Action}}
\;\xrightarrow{\;C_{\text{UX}}\;}
\;\underset{\text{analog}}{\text{Experience}}
\]

Each arrow is a serialization boundary. Each $C$ is a codebook that 
does not cross with the data. Drift rates are heterogeneous:

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Boundary} & \textbf{Transition} & \textbf{Drift} 
& \textbf{Typical verification} \\
\midrule
Sensor & Analog $\to$ digital & Slow (months) & Calibration \\
Model  & Digital $\to$ digital & Medium (weeks) & Monitoring \\
Agent  & Digital $\to$ digital & Fast (per-decision) & Reward audit \\
UX     & Digital $\to$ analog & Fastest & Almost never \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

A system verifying at the sensor boundary but not the UX boundary 
is Case~5 at the slowest-drift boundary and Case~1 at the fastest.

\textbf{Composite bound.} For a chain of $n$ boundaries with 
individual entropies $H_1, \ldots, H_n$, each operating under 
verification Case $c_i \in \{1, \ldots, 5\}$, the total unresolved 
entropy is:
\[
H_{\text{chain}} = \sum_{i : c_i < 5} H_i
\]
Only boundaries at Case~5 contribute zero. The dominant term is the 
highest-entropy unmonitored boundary. For most deployed systems, the 
UX boundary is simultaneously the highest-drift, highest-entropy, 
and least-monitored --- Case~1 at the point of maximum exposure.

Whether the individual boundary entropies interact (correlations 
between sensor codebook and model codebook reduce the joint space) 
or compound independently is an open question 
(Section~\ref{sec:open}).

\section{Metamathematical Note}
\label{sec:metamath}
%% ============================================================

Every mathematical framework that operates over a state space has an 
implicit precondition: the variables correspond to quantities in the 
world. Call this the \emph{referential integrity precondition}. The 
serialization boundary violates it universally. From the boundary, 
one derives any formalism's failure mode mechanically:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Identify the formalism's state space and its referential 
    integrity precondition.
    \item Introduce a serialization boundary: state transmits, 
    codebook does not.
    \item Apply the closure property: no function of the transmitted 
    state recovers the codebook.
    \item Derive the formalism-specific consequence.
\end{enumerate}

This paper applies the procedure rigorously to Bellman 
(Lemma~\ref{thm:bellman}) and Kuramoto 
(Proposition~\ref{prop:strogatz}). Section~\ref{sec:fault-line} 
sketches its application to Nash, Kalman, and Shannon. Full 
derivations for these three are conjectured applications; rigorous 
treatment is future work.

The claim is not depth within any single formalism --- the proofs 
are short. The claim is universality: the boundary is where every 
formalism's floor gives way, and it gives way for the same reason 
every time.

\toprule
\textbf{Formalism} & \textbf{What the agent lacks} \\
\midrule
POMDP & Observation of a known variable ($\omega \in S$, unobserved) \\
HMS Unawareness & Expressibility of a proposition ($\omega$ outside 
language) \\
Misspecification & Correct parameters within a fixed state space \\
Dec-POMDP & Another agent's private observation \\
\midrule
\textbf{This paper} & A dimension in its state space, destroyed by

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The distinction from model misspecification is precise: 
misspecification means the dimension is in the state space with wrong 
parameters. Null uncertainty means the dimension is not in the state 
space at all. Specification tests (e.g., Hausman) require variation 
across the parameter. When the parameter does not exist in $S_2$, 
there is no variation to detect.

\textbf{Data integration, schema matching, and ontology alignment} 
have addressed codebook mismatch for decades~\cite{rahm2001, 
euzenat2007, goh1999}. Their shared precondition is that both 
codebooks are accessible --- surfaced as schemas, ontologies, or 
formal context axioms --- before reconciliation begins. At boundaries 
where codebooks have not been surfaced, these tools have nothing to 
operate on. A practitioner may ask: ``Why isn't this just what ETL 
engineers do?'' The answer: ETL engineers perform informal rotations 
at boundaries they know about. They cannot inventory boundaries they 
do not know about, make their verifications machine-readable, or 
scale to agent-paced operation. This paper formalizes the informal 
process, makes its outputs computable, and identifies the structural 
limits of within-system diagnosis.

\section{Anticipated Objections}
\label{sec:objections}
%% ============================================================

\textbf{Objection 1: ``Bellman isn't breaking. This is model 
misspecification.''}

Model misspecification means a dimension is in the state space 
with wrong parameters: temperature is a variable in your model but 
with the wrong prior. Null uncertainty means the dimension is not 
in the state space at all: temperature is not a variable because 
serialization destroyed it. The distinction is testable. Residual 
analysis and specification tests (e.g., Hausman) detect 
misspecification because the misspecified parameter produces 
variation in the residuals. When the parameter does not exist in 
$S_2$, there is no variation to detect. Every diagnostic returns 
``everything is fine.'' 

\emph{Challenge:} Produce a specification test that detects a 
variable absent from the state space.

\textbf{Objection 2: ``The data integration literature already 
covers this.''}

Schema matching~\cite{rahm2001} requires both schemas. Ontology 
alignment~\cite{euzenat2007} requires both ontologies. Context 
interchange~\cite{goh1999} requires formal context axioms from 
both sides. These frameworks address codebook mismatch when both 
codebooks are surfaced. The specific contribution of this paper is 
the formalization of what happens when codebooks have not been 
surfaced --- the runtime condition where the mismatch is invisible 
because neither side has articulated their codebook, and no 
within-system diagnostic can detect the absence.

\emph{Challenge:} Produce the prior formalization of unsurfaced 
codebook mismatch at runtime serialization boundaries.

\textbf{Objection 3: ``The lemma is trivial. The formalism is 
disproportionate.''}

The lemma is trivial. That is the point. A two-line proof that 
cannot be argued with is stronger than a deep proof with 
assumptions to attack. The contribution is not the depth of any 
single derivation but the universality: the same two-line proof 
derives the failure mode for Bellman (Lemma~\ref{thm:bellman}), 
Kuramoto (Proposition~\ref{prop:strogatz}), and --- via the 
four-step traversal procedure (Section~\ref{sec:metamath}) --- for 
any formalism with a referential integrity precondition. The 
five-column gauge is not an analogy. It is a running protocol 
with a W3C community group, dereferenceable URIs, and a reference 
implementation~\cite{itelman2026lh}.

\textbf{Objection 4: ``The 7.22 bits is stipulated, not derived.''}

Correct. The specific $|\Omega_i|$ values are design choices, 
acknowledged in Section~\ref{sec:answer}. The claim is not that 
7.22 is derived from first principles. The claim is that 
$H_{\text{total}} > 0$ for any plausible reading of this 
stimulus: any analyst examining \texttt{4/5/2026} in a CSV with no 
metadata will agree $|\Omega| \geq 2$. The specific value is an 
instantiation. The bound's existence is a consequence of the 
closure property, not of the enumeration.

\textbf{Objection 5: ``Just model it as a POMDP with $\omega$ in the 
state space.''}

Adding the codebook variable to the state space is the solution. 
Knowing you need to add it is the problem. Modeling the boundary as 
a POMDP with $\omega \in S$ presupposes that the agent knows $\omega$ 
exists --- which is precisely what null uncertainty forecloses. The 
agent at Layer~2 has no slot for $\omega$, no signal that $\omega$ 
was ever relevant, and no procedure that surfaces the need for it 
from within $S_2$. The POMDP reformulation is correct \emph{after} 
the rotation has been performed. It is unavailable before.

\emph{Challenge:} Produce the procedure that tells the agent which 
variables to add to its state space, from within $S_2$.

\textbf{Objection 6: ``The experiment is a demonstration, not a 
test.''}

Correct. The paper states this explicitly: both demonstrations are 
proofs of mechanism, not proofs of claim. The empirical contribution 
is not the CSV. It is the reference implementation 
(Section~\ref{sec:implementation}): a running instrument that 
computes $H_{\text{total}}$ in real time, scores ADUH per field, 
catches hallucinations live, and matches the pre-computed answer key 
on every run. Production validation across real system boundaries is 
future work. The instrument exists. The measurement is the work.

\section{Falsification Criteria}
%% ============================================================

\textbf{Null uncertainty:}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Produce a function of the received CSV alone that determines 
  the sender's date format.
  \item Demonstrate that the Layer~2 agent can detect the date was 
  ambiguous, given only Layer~1's JSON.
  \item Show $\Delta = 0$ for all configuration pairs.
  \item Produce a Layer~2 agent with date accuracy above 50\% without 
  access to the original CSV.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Hallucination:}
\begin{enumerate}[resume]
  \item Produce a Layer~2 agent that identifies which fields have no 
  referent in $W$, from the JSON alone.
  \item Produce a within-system audit that bounds $|H|$ without 
  querying the originating system.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{D/U equivalence:}
\begin{enumerate}[resume]
  \item Produce a Layer~2 epistemic state that differs between a 
  $D$-on-all pipeline and a $U$-on-all pipeline, given identical 
  serialized outputs.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Open Questions}
\label{sec:open}
%% ============================================================

\begin{question}[Recursive Boundary Detection]
Does there exist a function $g: S \to \{0, 1\}$ computable from $s$ 
alone such that $g(s) = 1$ iff $|\Omega| \leq R$?
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Bellman with a Boundary Term]
\[
\tilde{V}(s) = \max_{a \in A} \left[ R(s, a) - \lambda\, 
H(\Omega | s) + \gamma\, \tilde{V}(s') \right]
\]
Is this well-defined? Does it converge?
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Minimum Viable Provenance and the Converse Theorem]
What is the minimum number of bits that must cross the serialization 
boundary to prevent Bellman failure? A tight lower bound would be the 
structural parallel to Shannon's noisy coding theorem. The entropy 
bound $H_{\text{total}}$ is a candidate. Whether it is tight --- 
both necessary and sufficient --- is the open question that would 
complete the structural parallel to information theory.
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Coupling Strength and the Rotation Threshold]
Does there exist a Kuramoto-type model for decision coherence in 
which the rotation cost plays the role of coupling strength? Is there 
a critical rotation rate above which agent networks achieve codebook 
coherence?
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Hallucination-Adjusted Coverage]
$C^* = |E| / (N + |H|)$ requires knowing $|H|$, which is 
uncomputable from within $S_2$. Does there exist an audit procedure 
that bounds $|H|$ from outside the pipeline?
\end{question}

\begin{question}[The Full Reality Chain]
The sensor-to-user pipeline contains boundaries with heterogeneous 
drift rates. Is there a composite entropy bound for the full chain? 
For the UX boundary: is the codebook of human interpretation 
expressible in a form that admits $|\Omega|$ enumeration?
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Traversal Completeness]
Is the referential integrity precondition the unique shared 
precondition across all formalisms that fail at the serialization 
boundary? Can it be axiomatized independently, producing a 
metatheory from which all formalism-specific results follow?
\end{question}

\section{Conclusion}
%% ============================================================

The Bellman equation's preconditions for meaningful operation are 
destroyed at the serialization boundary between agents. The mechanism 
is serialization itself: writing assumptions into structured data 
collapses ambiguity into false certainty, severs provenance, and 
simultaneously truncates the downstream state space of real dimensions 
and populates it with fictitious ones.

The primary contributions are the closure property as the 
load-bearing claim, the ADUH scoring taxonomy as its empirical 
instrument, the D/U equivalence result as its most non-obvious 
consequence, and the Strogatz Inversion as its dynamic extension. 
The converse theorem --- a tight lower bound on cross-boundary 
provenance --- is the next result.

The reference implementation demonstrates that the instrument runs, 
the predictions hold, and the problem is not theoretical. Production 
validation --- applying the ADUH framework to real system boundaries 
--- is the natural next step. The instrument exists. The measurement 
is the work.

All claims carry falsification criteria. The abandonment conditions 
are stated. We invite correction.
